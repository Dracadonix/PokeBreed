A Comprehensive Professional Compendium | Research Lead: Lime | 2024-2026 Archive

The pursuit of excellence within the Pokémon ecosystem has centered upon the rigorous discipline of breeding. While the casual observer may perceive breeding as a simple mechanic, the professional understands it as a complex synthesis of genetic manipulation and statistical probability.

The Biological Framework: Egg Groups

Compatibility remains governed by the rigid biological classification of Egg Groups. For an egg to be produced, the two parents must share at least one Egg Group and be of opposite genders, or one parent must be a Ditto.

Egg Moves: The Mirror Herb Revolution

The Mirror Herb allows for immediate transfer of an Egg Move during a Picnic. Protocol: The target must have an empty slot, hold the herb, and share a picnic with a donor knowing the move. Note: Mirror moves cannot be recovered via Move Reminder.

Logistical Optimization: The Sandwich Meta

Efficiency is achieved through Egg Power buffs. Level 2 (Peanut Butter/Banana) is the standard for mass breeding. Level 3 (Herba Mystica) is reserved for high-stakes Shiny hunting.

By using parents from different regional languages (e.g., Japanese Ditto + English Charizard), the shiny rate is permanently boosted to 1/683, or 1/512 with the Shiny Charm.

Historical Mechanics: Johto to Paldea

In Gen II, the Deter Values (DVs) were inherited directly from the opposite-gender parent. In Gen V, Hidden Abilities (HA) were gender-locked to females. Today, Ditto allows any gender to pass an HA with a 60% probability.

The Psychology of Mastery

The professional must resist the "Sunk Cost Fallacy." Every 4-IV hatch is obsolete data once a 5-IV hatch occurs. The Nursery Swap must be ruthless. Mastery is the conversion of random chance into precision science.

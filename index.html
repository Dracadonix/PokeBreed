The Breeding Bible: OMNI-REDOX

The pursuit of excellence within the Pokémon ecosystem has centered upon the rigorous discipline of breeding. While the casual observer perceives breeding as a simple mechanic, the professional understands it as a synthesis of genetic manipulation and logistical optimization.

I. Biological Framework

Pokémon are classified into fifteen distinct Egg Groups. For an egg to be produced, the two parents must share an Egg Group. The offspring manifestations are dictated by the maternal lineage.

II. Statistical Inheritance

Individual Values (IVs) range from 0 to 31. The Destiny Knot guarantees that 5 of the parents' combined 12 IVs will be passed to the offspring. This allows breeders to statistically "lock in" near-perfection.

III. Tactical Minimalism (0-IV Strategy)

In the VGC meta, "perfect" stats are not always 31. Professionals seek 0 IVs in Speed for Trick Room conditions or 0 IVs in Attack to minimize damage from Foul Play or Confusion.

IV. Nature and the Everstone

The Everstone provides a 100% guarantee that the parent holding it will pass its Nature. This is essential for competitive builds like Jolly, Timid, or Adamant.

V. The Mirror Herb Revolution

Generation IX introduced the Mirror Herb. By having an empty move slot and holding the herb during a Picnic, moves can be transferred instantly between species, bypassing days of chain-breeding.

VI. Shiny Mathematics

The Masuda Method (breeding parents of different languages) increases shiny rolls to 1/683. With the Shiny Charm, this drops to 1/512. Statistically, 90% probability is reached after 1,178 eggs.

VII. Culinary Logistics

Egg Power Level 2: Banana + Peanut Butter + Butter. This is the professional standard for resource-to-result efficiency in Paldea.

VIII. The Professional Mindset

The breeder must be ruthless. Once a 5-IV specimen is hatched, every 4-IV specimen is obsolete data. Efficiency is not just about the game—it is about the time of the practitioner.
